Maps

Notebook1 Notebook2

The following map reprsent all the stations of the Hubway network in Boston Urban Area. The size of the dots are plotted proportionaly to the amount of mouvement at the station over the past two years, with a minimum threshold for vizualisation purpose. The links are shaded according to the occurence of trips between two stations (origin and destination). No threshold, so if no link, the occurence is very low compared to the most used OD pairs. We can notice that the most used stations are aggregated in two specific areas, wich are Cambridge (between MIT and Harvard) and Downtown Boston (Central). In those areas, the intensity of the links is the highest as well, and the length of the trips is quite low.

Eco-totem count

Notebook

The ecototem data come from a cyclist counter located on broadway, Cambridge. It returns every 15 minutes how many people passed by the counter, in both directions (Eastbound is toward Boston downtown, Westbound is toward Cambridge/Somerville). In addition to the seasonal variability of bicycle use, the intersting plots are when you display the time serie one a week basis and on a daily basis. On the week basis, we can see the high variability in the amount of cyclists between the weekend and the working days, meaning that a lot of rides are commuting trips. On the day basis, by selecting the "all trips" button we can see the total amount of cyclists as well as the separated count between East and West. We see That is the morning peak, a large majority of cyclists go to Boston, and the opposite in the evening.

Exploration of Google Map information

Notebook

From the Google Maps API Distance Matrix, I requested the trip duration and trip distances to populate OD matrix between all my ID stations in order to be able to compare the actual speed of cyclists while using the Hubway bikes and the speed that googlemaps would returns. It ends-up with a different distribution of speed as well as a much lower average speed. For the record, most of the trips are less than three km, in urban areas. So an overestimation of 5 km/h over 3 km is approximatively a 6 minutes difference. Further investigation could be made with these data, for instance measuring the diference between car trip duration and bicycle trip duration during peak hours.
